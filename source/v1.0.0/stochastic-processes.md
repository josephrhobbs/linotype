# Stochastic Processes

~

From the time of Isaac Newton until the middle of the nineteenth century, the study and philosophy of physics followed predictable, _deterministic_, _reversible_ patterns.  Newtonian mechanics is, itself, time-reversible.  That is, Newtonian systems can evolve _backwards in time_ under the same rules as forwards in time.  Consider the classic example of an elastic collision, perhaps of two billiard balls.  The video of an elastic collision played in reverse looks just like an elastic collision played forward.

In the late nineteenth and early twentieth centuries, the predominant perspective on physics as consisting of reversible phenomena began to shift dramatically.  It became clear to the leading minds of the day that _physics is, at its heart, composed of irreversible phenomena_.
